Research Assumptions & Expected Insights

The Innovation Reality Pulse is designed not only to collect data, but to test a series of assumptions about how organizations across the MENA region approach innovation — and to uncover the patterns that explain its current state.

Research Assumptions

Five Insights We Expect to Uncover

By combining responses across multiple dimensions — how innovation is defined, why organizations innovate, the barriers they face, and how innovation systems operate in practice — the survey aims to generate 4–5 powerful insights that shape the final report narrative.

1
Innovation Is Widely Recognized but Not Always Clearly Defined

While innovation is widely acknowledged as important for long-term growth, many organizations still lack a clear and shared definition of what it means in practice — often associating it with incremental improvements rather than a structured organizational capability.

What we expect to reveal
  • Most organizations acknowledge innovation as important, but only a minority have a structured approach to managing it.
Q6 — Innovation DefinitionQ11 — Innovation Structure
"Most organizations recognize innovation as important, but only a minority treat it as a structured capability."
2
Innovation Ambition May Exceed Current Organizational Capabilities

Many organizations aspire to use innovation as a driver of growth and differentiation, yet their current systems and practices may still be oriented toward incremental improvements — revealing a gap between ambition and capability.

What we expect to reveal
  • Many companies expect innovation to drive future growth, yet most innovation efforts remain focused on incremental improvements.
Q7 — Innovation PurposeQ11 — Innovation Structure
"Organizations aspire to strategic innovation but operate primarily in incremental mode."
"Organizations expect innovation to fuel future growth, yet most efforts remain focused on improving the core business."
3
Innovation Barriers Are More Cultural and Structural Than Technical

The biggest barriers to innovation are rarely technical. They tend to stem from cultural dynamics, governance models, or decision-making structures — not a shortage of ideas or technology.

What we expect to reveal
  • The biggest obstacles are likely to be risk aversion, slow decisions, and leadership alignment — not technology or ideas.
Q8 — Innovation BarriersQ9 — Open Quotes
"Innovation challenges are rarely technical — they are cultural and structural."
"Risk aversion and slow decision cycles remain the most common obstacles to innovation."
4
Leaders Understand What Makes Innovation Work

Most leaders and practitioners already have a strong intuition about the elements required to build an effective innovation system — yet these elements are not consistently embedded in practice.

What we expect to reveal
  • Executives generally understand the ingredients of successful innovation systems: strategic clarity, leadership sponsorship, and strong insights.
Q10 — System PrioritiesQ11 — Innovation Structure
"Leaders know what innovation requires — but organizations struggle to implement it."
5
The Real Challenge Is Turning Ideas into Impact

The biggest difficulty organizations face is not idea generation, but execution and scaling — consistent with what many companies experience across markets and industries.

What we expect to reveal
  • The biggest difficulty is likely not idea generation, but execution and scaling.

Methodology

How the Results Will Be Analysed

Once data collection is completed, responses will be aggregated and analysed to identify patterns across organizations and industries. The analysis will focus on four main lenses.

Innovation Positioning

How organizations frame the role of innovation in their strategy — drawn from the definition and purpose questions.

Innovation Barriers

Barrier selections aggregated to identify the most common obstacles organizations face when trying to innovate.

Innovation System Priorities

What leaders believe are the key drivers of innovation success — highlighting the most critical elements identified.

Segmentation

Where sufficient data is available, results may be segmented by industry, geography, or respondent role to uncover additional patterns.

Study Scope

Respondent Profile & Target Audience

The Innovation Reality Pulse targets professionals involved in innovation-related activities across organizations operating in the MENA region — from business units and marketing to insights, R&D, strategy, and corporate innovation teams.

Participation is open across company sizes and organizational levels, capturing perspectives from both leadership and operational roles. While all industries are welcome, the primary focus of the initial analysis will be on FMCG and Food & Beverage sectors, where innovation plays a critical role in portfolio growth and competitiveness.

Company-Level Output

Company-Level Benchmark Snapshot

When multiple responses are collected from the same organization, survey results can be aggregated to generate a light benchmarking snapshot — comparing that company's responses with the broader regional or industry sample.

This snapshot provides a high-level view of how the organization's innovation system compares with peers and highlights areas that may deserve deeper exploration. While directional in nature, it can serve as a useful starting point for internal discussion and help organizations identify areas that could benefit from a deeper innovation maturity assessment.

The snapshot may include:

A directional view across five innovation dimensions — useful as a starting point for internal discussion.

Innovation Positioning — how the organization defines innovation vs. the regional benchmark.
Innovation Barrier Profile — most common internal barriers vs. those reported across the segment.
Innovation System Overview — comparison across the six key innovation system dimensions.
Innovation Gap Indicators — areas where the gap between perceived importance and current practice exceeds the regional average.
Visual Benchmark Summary — radar charts or side-by-side comparisons illustrating positioning vs. peers.
Research Goal

What This Study Ultimately Aims to Achieve

By exploring these assumptions through a combination of structured questions and practitioner reflections, the Innovation Reality Pulse aims to generate a clearer understanding of how innovation systems operate across the region.

The goal is not to evaluate individual organizations, but to identify shared patterns, tensions, and opportunities that can inform more effective innovation practices in the future. Ultimately, the insights generated will serve as a foundation for deeper discussions with organizations interested in strengthening their innovation capabilities.
